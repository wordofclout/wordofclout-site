Quick Answer: Vertical videos are filmed in portrait orientation (9:16 aspect ratio) for mobile viewing. They matter because 90%+ of social media is consumed on mobile, and platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts prioritize this format.

What are vertical videos and why do they matter?

Short answer: Vertical videos fill mobile screens and dominate social media engagement.

Full Explanation

What Is Vertical Video?

  • Aspect ratio: 9:16 (portrait orientation)
  • Resolution: Typically 1080x1920 pixels
  • Duration: Usually 15-60 seconds
  • Platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat

Why Vertical Matters

1. Mobile-First Consumption

  • 90%+ of social media accessed via mobile
  • Vertical fills the entire screen
  • No rotation required from viewers
2. Higher Engagement
  • Vertical videos get 40% more engagement than horizontal
  • Full-screen immersion increases watch time
  • Platform algorithms favor native formats
3. Platform Priority All major platforms now prioritize vertical:
  • TikTok (native vertical)
  • Instagram Reels
  • YouTube Shorts
  • Facebook Reels
  • Snapchat

How to Create Vertical Content

From scratch:

  • Film with phone held vertically
  • Frame subjects in center
  • Use vertical-first composition
From horizontal video:
  • Extract key moments
  • Reframe for 9:16
  • Add captions and text overlays
  • Use AI tools like RipurposeAI to automate

Best Practices

  • Hook viewers in first 1-2 seconds
  • Keep text in safe zones (not cut off)
  • Add captions (85% watch muted)
  • Optimize for thumbnail/cover image
